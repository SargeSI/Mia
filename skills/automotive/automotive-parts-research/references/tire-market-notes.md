## Key Tire Models — Test Summary

### Kumho Ecsta HS52 (Premium Touring)
- **Tests:** 8 tests, average 5th place. Best: 2/55 (AutoBild 2024), 4/21 (AutoBild 2024).
- **Strengths:** dry braking, wet braking, wear (ADAC 2024: 1st in wear, 1st in value/cost-per-km).
- **Weaknesses:** aquaplaning (especially curved), rolling resistance, inconsistent noise.
- **Verdict:** Best value touring tire. Excellent safety metrics. Only hesitation: heavy rain highway driving.

### Hankook Ventus Prime 4 K135 (Premium Touring)
- **Tests:** 16 tests, average 6th place.
- **Strengths:** dry grip (top-tier braking/handling), fuel efficiency.
- **Weaknesses:** aquaplaning resistance (straight + curved) — frequently bottom quartile, sometimes last. Inconsistent noise.
- **Verdict:** Strong if aquaplaning isn't a concern. Otherwise, HS52 beats it.

### Nexen N'Fera Sport (UHP)
- **Tests:** AutoBild 2026 "Good", ADAC 2025 "Good", TCS 2025 "Very Recommendable". AutoBild sportscars 2025: **1st in aquaplaning resistance**, 2nd in wet handling. AutoBild 2026: 9/10 wet lateral aquaplaning.
- **OE fitments:** Porsche Panamera (Gen 2), Porsche Cayenne (2024, all variants except Turbo GT), VW Golf 8, SEAT Leon. See `references/nexen-oe-fitments.md`.
- **Strengths:** dry braking, wet braking, aquaplaning resistance (#1 in class), rolling resistance, pass-by noise, mileage.
- **Weaknesses:** 91 load index (no XL in 225/45 R17). For vehicles under ~1500 kg this is acceptable.
- **Verdict:** Best wet-weather UHP in its price range. The Porsche OE reference is a strong quality signal — Porsche's OE approval process heavily weights wet safety. If the car is primarily for rainy conditions, this is the top pick among affordable UHP tires.

### Nexen N'Fera SU1 (UHP, older model)
- **Tests:** tire-reviews.com summary: *«professional tests consistently place it behind newer rivals for wet braking, wet handling and aquaplaning safety»*.
- **Strengths:** dry grip, value pricing, XL availability.
- **Weaknesses:** wet grip, wet braking, aquaplaning — all behind current competitors.
- **Verdict:** NOT for wet-weather use. Budget UHP for dry conditions only. Was superseded by N'Fera Sport which fixes the wet weaknesses.

### Kumho Ecsta PS71 (UHP)
- **Tests:** 15 tests, clear wet-weather bias. Podiums: 4/21 (AutoBild 2025), 4/52 (AB shootout 2025), 5/21 (AutoBild 2023), 3/17 (ADAC 2021).
- **Strengths:** wet braking, wet handling, aquaplaning — repeatedly top-tier.
- **Weaknesses:** dry performance mid-pack, NVH poor, wear inconsistent, rolling resistance high.
- **Verdict:** Best wet-weather UHP in its class. Trade-off: noise and wear.

### Pirelli Cinturato P7 (Premium Touring)
- **Tests:** 27 tests. Auto Express 2024 (225/45 R17): 6/8 overall, 99% score. 1st in rolling resistance, 2nd in cabin noise.
- **Strengths:** fuel efficiency, comfort, low noise.
- **Weaknesses:** surprisingly poor wet AND dry grip for a premium tire. Long braking distances.
- **Verdict:** Comfort/efficiency tire. Not for safety-first selection. Overpriced relative to grip performance.

### Cordiant Sport 3 (Russian UHP)
- **Tests:** No independent European tests. Russian reviews positive on wet grip, wear, noise.
- **Load/Speed:** 94V — V (240 km/h) adequate for most applications.
- **Verdict:** Budget Russian option. Acceptable if budget-constrained, but no verification from independent tests.

### Nexen N'Fera Primus (V / QX) (Premium Touring)
- **Tests (Primus V):** 7 tests, average 11.6. ADAC 2024: 11/16 ("Satisfactory"). AutoBild 2024: 17/55 ("Satisfactory").
- **Strengths:** wet braking occasionally competitive.
- **Weaknesses:** wear (repeatedly bottom), dry braking/handling poor, rolling resistance below average.
- **Primus QX:** Replacement model, no tests yet. Unknown quantity — budget play with risk.
- **Verdict:** Skip Primus V. QX — only if budget is primary and risk is acceptable.
