# Sgroppino

Venetian cocktail-dessert, eaten with a spoon. Halfway between cocktail, sorbet, and sparkling wine.

## Classic
- 2-3 scoops lemon sorbet
- 30 ml vodka (or grappa, but vodka is more neutral)
- 100 ml Prosecco Brut (dry is critical — sorbet is already sweet)

## Limoncello variant
- Replace vodka with 25-30 ml limoncello — more lemony, slightly sweeter
- Prosecco **MUST be Brut** to compensate for limoncello's sweetness

**Proportions** (per serving):
- 2 scoops lemon sorbet
- 25-30 ml limoncello (or 30 ml vodka for classic)
- 100 ml Prosecco Brut

**Technique:**
1. Chill glasses in freezer 5-10 min
2. In bowl: sorbet + limoncello → whisk until creamy (sorbet softened but not fully melted)
3. Pour Prosecco in thin stream while whisking — mixture will foam up
4. Pour into chilled glasses
5. Serve with a spoon — sgroppino is eaten, not drunk

**Prosecco selection:**
- **Brut only** (dry) — Extra Dry/Dry will make it cloying
- Budget picks (differences vanish in the mix): Martini Prosecco Brut (~1000-1300₽), Canti Brut (~900-1100₽), Zonin Brut (~800-1000₽)
- Don't waste Franciacorta or Champagne — nuances are lost
